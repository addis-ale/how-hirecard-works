# How Cards Are Generated - Client Guide

This document explains in simple terms how each of the 13 hiring cards is created for your job posting.

---

## üéØ Overview

When you submit a job URL or description, our system:

1. **Scrapes** the job posting to extract key information
2. **Searches** for similar jobs and potential candidates
3. **Analyzes** market data (salaries, competition, talent availability)
4. **Generates** 13 strategic cards using AI analysis
5. **Displays** them in a beautiful, interactive deck

The entire process takes about 2 minutes, and you'll see cards appear one by one as they're generated.

---

## üìä The 4 Card Groups

Cards are generated in **4 groups**, with each group building on the previous one:

### **GROUP 1: Job Analysis Cards** (5 cards)

_Generated from your job posting + similar job descriptions_

These cards analyze **what you're hiring for**:

- What the role actually does
- What skills are needed
- How to message the role
- What type of person fits
- How to reach out to candidates

**Data Used:** Your job description, requirements, company info, and similar job postings from LinkedIn, Indeed, and Glassdoor.

---

### **GROUP 2: People Analysis Cards** (1 card)

_Generated from candidate profile data_

This card shows **where to find candidates**:

- Which companies your ideal candidates work at
- Career progression patterns
- Where talent is moving

**Data Used:** LinkedIn candidate profiles matching your role requirements.

---

### **GROUP 3: Combined Analysis Cards** (4 cards)

_Generated from job + people + market data_

These cards combine everything to show **market reality**:

- How competitive the market is
- What salary to offer
- How many candidates you need to reach out to
- Overall feasibility score (0-10)

**Data Used:** Your job posting, similar jobs, candidate data, salary data from Glassdoor, and industry benchmarks.

---

### **GROUP 4: Strategy Cards** (3 cards)

_Generated from all previous cards_

These cards provide **actionable execution plans**:

- Interview process design
- Evaluation scorecard
- Week-by-week hiring roadmap

**Data Used:** Synthesizes insights from all previous cards.

---

## üé¥ Detailed Card Breakdown

### **1. Reality Card** ‚≠ê

**Purpose:** Master feasibility score and what helps/hurts your hire

**How It's Generated:**

- Analyzes all other cards (Role, Skill, Market, Pay, Funnel)
- Calculates a 0-10 feasibility score
- Identifies what's working in your favor
- Highlights major obstacles

**What You Get:**

- Reality Score (0-10 scale)
- Market conditions summary
- Helping factors (what's working)
- Hurting factors (challenges)
- Critical risks and fixes

**Example Score Meaning:**

- 0-3: Nearly impossible
- 4-5: Very difficult
- 6-7: Challenging but doable
- 8-9: Reasonable
- 10: Easy

---

### **2. Role Card**

**Purpose:** Define what the person will do and success criteria

**How It's Generated:**

- Analyzes your job description
- Compares with similar job postings to improve clarity
- Extracts role mission, outcomes, and responsibilities
- Identifies what makes this role unique

**What You Get:**

- Role summary (2 sentences)
- Role mission (what they own)
- 5 success outcomes (first 6-12 months)
- What great looks like (6 characteristics)
- Tools/technologies they'll work with
- What they WON'T do (important boundaries)
- Improved job description (before/after comparison)
- Red flags and fixes

**Data Sources:** Your job posting + similar job descriptions from Glassdoor, LinkedIn, Indeed

---

### **3. Market Card**

**Purpose:** Supply vs demand analysis - how competitive is the market

**How It's Generated:**

- Counts similar jobs on LinkedIn, Indeed, Glassdoor
- Counts available candidates from LinkedIn
- Calculates candidates per job ratio
- Determines market tightness (Tight/Balanced/Loose)

**What You Get:**

- Talent availability (total, qualified, employed, open to work)
- Supply vs demand ratios
- Market tightness assessment
- Geographic distribution
- Market insights and opportunities
- Recommendations based on market conditions

**Data Sources:** LinkedIn job search, Indeed job search, LinkedIn candidate search

---

### **4. Skill Card**

**Purpose:** Extract and categorize required skills

**How It's Generated:**

- Extracts skills from your job description
- Compares with similar jobs to identify additional skills
- Categorizes into technical, product, and behavioral
- Identifies must-haves vs nice-to-haves

**What You Get:**

- 5 technical skills
- 4 product skills
- 4 behavioral skills
- 4 must-have skills
- 4 upskillable skills (can learn on the job)
- Red flags and fixes

**Data Sources:** Your job requirements + similar job descriptions to identify commonly required skills

---

### **5. Pay Card**

**Purpose:** Compensation analysis using real market data

**How It's Generated:**

- Extracts salary from your job posting (if available)
- Analyzes salary data from 400+ similar jobs
- Includes Glassdoor median salary data
- Calculates percentiles (25th, 50th, 75th, 90th)

**What You Get:**

- Market compensation percentiles
- Recommended salary range
- Glassdoor median (if available)
- Honest assessment of your offer competitiveness
- Red flags and fixes
- Timeline to failure (when comp kills offers)

**Data Sources:** Glassdoor salary data, similar job postings with salary info

---

### **6. Funnel Card**

**Purpose:** Calculate outreach volume needed using industry benchmarks

**How It's Generated:**

- Uses market tightness from Market Card
- Applies real industry benchmarks:
  - Applicants per hire
  - Phone screen pass rate
  - Onsite pass rate
  - Offer accept rate
- Calculates funnel stages backwards from 1 hire

**What You Get:**

- Complete funnel breakdown:
  - Outreach needed
  - Expected replies
  - Phone screens
  - Interviews
  - Offers
  - Hires (1)
- Conversion rates at each stage
- Time to hire estimate
- Funnel health comparison
- Red flags and fixes

**Data Sources:** Industry benchmarks, market analysis

---

### **7. Fit Card**

**Purpose:** Understand candidate motivations and what makes them say yes/no

**How It's Generated:**

- Analyzes role characteristics (remote/hybrid, team size, autonomy)
- Identifies candidate persona archetype
- Determines what motivates this persona
- Identifies turn-offs

**What You Get:**

- Candidate persona description
- What motivates them (5-6 factors)
- What they avoid (4-5 turn-offs)
- How candidates evaluate this role
- Why they accept/decline offers
- Red flags and fixes

**Data Sources:** Psychographic research, industry reports, DISC profiles

---

### **8. Message Card**

**Purpose:** How to pitch the role to get responses

**How It's Generated:**

- Uses role mission from Role Card
- Identifies unique value propositions
- Creates attention-grabbing hooks
- Develops messaging templates

**What You Get:**

- Core pitch (2-sentence value proposition)
- 3 scroll-stoppers (attention-grabbing hooks)
- 2 message templates
- Why messaging fails for this role
- Critical messaging improvements
- Hidden bottlenecks

**Data Sources:** Hiring research, EVP docs, competitor career pages

---

### **9. Outreach Card**

**Purpose:** Ready-to-send email and DM templates

**How It's Generated:**

- Uses core pitch from Message Card
- Creates 3-step outreach sequence
- Develops cold email templates
- Includes follow-up messages

**What You Get:**

- Introduction (outreach strategy context)
- Message 1: Cold outreach email (150 words)
- Message 2: Follow-up message (100 words)
- Message 3: Alternative approach for passive candidates
- Red flags and fixes
- Timeline to failure (when bad outreach kills pipeline)

**Data Sources:** Hiring research, EVP docs, competitor career pages

---

### **10. Talent Map Card**

**Purpose:** Pinpoint where top talent is moving next

**How It's Generated:**

- Analyzes LinkedIn candidate profiles
- Extracts current and previous companies
- Identifies top companies with most candidates
- Maps career progression patterns

**What You Get:**

- Primary feeder companies (6-8 companies where most candidates work)
- Secondary feeder companies (4-6 companies with fewer candidates)
- Companies to avoid (3 companies that might not be good fits)
- Talent flow map (career progression patterns)
- Candidate persona insights
- Red flags and fixes

**Data Sources:** LinkedIn candidate profiles (real data, not made up)

---

### **11. Interview Card**

**Purpose:** Design the interview process and assessment questions

**How It's Generated:**

- Uses must-have skills from Skill Card
- Uses key outcomes from Role Card
- Designs optimal interview loop
- Creates signal questions

**What You Get:**

- Optimal interview loop (4 stages with what to assess)
- Signal questions (4 key questions for top skills)
- What kills interview processes
- Red flags and fixes

**Data Sources:** Interview playbooks, best practices, industry frameworks

---

### **12. Scorecard Card**

**Purpose:** Standardized criteria to reduce hiring bias

**How It's Generated:**

- Uses competencies from Skill Card
- Uses interview stages from Interview Card
- Creates evaluation framework
- Defines what good looks like

**What You Get:**

- 5-6 key competencies to evaluate
- Evaluation criteria (what good looks like for each)
- Red flags and fixes

**Data Sources:** Interview guides, external frameworks, recruiter inputs

---

### **13. Plan Card**

**Purpose:** Week-by-week hiring execution roadmap

**How It's Generated:**

- Synthesizes insights from all previous cards
- Creates actionable next steps
- Develops timeline based on market conditions
- Defines SLAs for each stage

**What You Get:**

- 5-6 immediate next steps
- Pre-launch checklist
- Realistic timeline based on market
- Service level agreements (SLAs) for each stage
- Red flags and fixes

**Data Sources:** Industry frameworks, best practices, recruiter knowledge

## üîç What Happens Behind the Scenes

### 1. Job Scraping

- Your job URL is scraped using ScrapingBee
- HTML is parsed to extract structured data
- AI extracts missing fields if needed

### 2. External Data Collection

- **Similar Jobs:** Searched on LinkedIn, Indeed, Glassdoor (400+ jobs)
- **Candidates:** LinkedIn People Search (up to 50 profiles)
- **Salaries:** Glassdoor salary data for your role/location
- **Benchmarks:** Industry hiring funnel benchmarks

### 3. Card Generation

- Each card uses specific data sources
- AI analyzes the data with structured prompts
- JSON responses are parsed and validated
- Cards are stored and returned to frontend

### 4. Error Handling

- If a card fails to generate, it returns `null`
- Frontend gracefully handles missing cards
- Process continues even if some cards fail
- Errors are logged for debugging

## üí° Key Insights

### Why Cards Are Generated in Groups

- **Group 1** can start immediately after scraping (only needs job data)
- **Group 2** needs candidate data (takes time to collect)
- **Group 3** needs both job + people + market data (most comprehensive)
- **Group 4** synthesizes everything (strategy and execution)

### Why Some Cards Depend on Others

- **Message Card** needs Role Card (to know what to pitch)
- **Outreach Card** needs Message Card (to create templates)
- **Funnel Card** needs Market Card (to know market tightness)
- **Reality Card** needs all previous cards (master synthesis)
- **Interview Card** needs Skill Card (to know what to assess)
- **Scorecard Card** needs Interview Card (to align evaluation)
- **Plan Card** needs multiple cards (to create comprehensive roadmap)

### Data Freshness

- Job data: Scraped fresh each time
- Similar jobs: Searched fresh each time
- Candidates: Searched fresh each time
- Salaries: Cached but refreshed periodically
- Benchmarks: Updated periodically
